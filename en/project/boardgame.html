Project overview and background

In early December 2020, I locked myself away for a week on the Opal Coast in France, in need of inspiration and fresh air. It was while I was leaving that I came up with the simple idea: “What if I created a board game in which the objective is to cook vegetable soups?” I then started a study of board games to find out what would characterize my game, what people look for when they play games, and what drives me in the act of playing. Three points emerged: interaction, strategy and fun.

From there, I began a creative process to find a winning formula or, if I may say so, the right recipe! So I came up with a card game in which the aim is to cook soups from the various ingredients you can obtain by “trading". As soon as the lockdown was over, I organized dozens of test games with all types of players to find out what worked and what needed to be improved. As time went by, I managed to develop the game into a first version that could be presented to professionals. On several occasions, I met and exchanged ideas with publishers, game fairs and specialists, such as at La Luck, a game bar.

Today, the game is functional and appreciated. I'm currently working on an exchange with a design school to build a graphic charter and bring artistic coherence.

If I could remember just one thing :

Listen & Observe: by having my game tested many times, I've learned the art of questioning, understanding testers' behaviors and, above all, empathizing with them to welcome their feedback and remarks with detachment.
