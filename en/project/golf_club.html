Project overview and background

Looking for a meaningful experience at Decathlon among my school internships, I went to the Inesis offices after golf practice on the driving range. There, I managed to meet Albert, the product manager in charge of golf clubs. Later, he explained that it was partly thanks to my spontaneous approach to him that he decided to find a place for me on his team. For ten weeks, I was in charge of launching the First Club project: a golf club adapted for beginners so that, from the very first bucket of ball on the driving range, they could watch their balls take off correctly while ensuring quality posture and gesture. In short, a first club that's both enjoyable and educational.

Based on this idea, I was completely autonomous. First, I had to make sure that potential users (people who'd never played golf as well as professionals teaching beginners) were interested in the project. I therefore met dozens of future players and local golf instructors. Everyone was clear: the First Club has educational and economic potential (Inesis is a company that sells products, so we had to make sure the club had buyers once the project developed).

Then I developed the club prototypes: which grip? which shaft? which club head? For the shaft, industrialization is external to Inesis, so there's no real innovation to be made as such. For the grips and clubheads, I had to determine which designs would be the most suitable: iron head, wood head, hybrid, classic grip, hexagonal grip to facilitate hand placement, putter grip to align the thumbs, XXL grip to force ample movement... The challenge was to decide which combination of grip and clubhead would constitute my final prototype. To determine this, I once again called on novice players to test the clubs and get their feedback. I organized several test sessions on the driving range to get as close as possible to the testers.

At the end of the internship, I presented my results and the progress I had made in two months to the entire Inesis team. They particularly appreciated the energy with which I had progressed on the project, and the creativity I had shown in designing the club prototypes. The team had been talking about the idea of a first club for a long time, and were also delighted to see the project finally come to reality..

If I could remember just one thing :

The corporate posture : this experience was the trigger for my porfessionalism. When I began the work experience that followed this one, I immediately felt part of the teams on site and considering myself a proper member of the company despite my intern status.
