Project overview and background

For this project, as is often the case when we have to choose a partner, I relied on my usual partner, Jules. Over the years, we've learned to organize ourselves and divide up the tasks. The previous class had told us about this programming project when they were working on it. We agreed with Jules that our aim would be not only to develop an ambitious game, but also to ensure that it was functional on the day of our presentation. The aim of the project was to develop an arcade game with, at our disposal, a matrix of 32x16 LEDs (a screen 32 pixels long by 16 wide), an Arduino board for coding, wiring and controls such as joysticks, sliders and push buttons.

We opted to develop Donkey Kong, an arcade game created in 1981 (and yes, 44 years already...), in which we played Mario, who must climb to the top of a tower to rescue a princess, taking care not to get knocked over by the barrels thrown by Donkey Kong.

We opted to develop Donkey Kong, an arcade game created in 1981 (and yes, 44 years already...), in which we played Mario, who must climb to the top of a tower to rescue a princess, taking care not to get knocked over by the barrels thrown by Donkey Kong. Although the game's scenario is relatively simple, certain aspects of the programming challenged us particularly :

  • Limited screen and offset setup: With only a 16 LED high screen, we had to find a way to display the rest of the course as the character moved up or down.
  • Movements and status changes: Depending on player actions or events, we had to imagine possible scenarios: what to do when a barrel hits the character? How do you prohibit or allow certain movements? What happens when the game is over?
  • Don't spread ourselves too thin: As we have an abundant imagination, we had to prioritize our ideas according to their relevance to the success of our project. For example, we could have proposed different levels of difficulty, added a microphone for sound effects, or designed a “duel” version in which two players would compete on the same course, with the winner being the one who reaches the last platform first.

If I could remember just one thing :

Taking the time to define the project: I think this IT project was particularly interesting for all the thinking we had to do. Even before we started coding the game, we had to schematize what the game would look like and what would be displayed on the screen, as well as considering all the events that might occur once the “console” was switched on.
