Project overview and background

As ITEEM is located in the Cité Scientifique in Villeneuve d'Ascq, as are the buildings of the Ecole Centrale de Lille, many of the courses I attended were held in these facilities. The foundry, the digital center, the electronics laboratories - these were all dedicated areas that gave us a real taste of the environments we'll be working in in the future. In a building dedicated to robotics, we were able to take many courses linked to non-linear systems, microcontrollers and digital simulation.

The project I was able to work on with my fellow pair projects, Jules, gave us the opportunity to use a great deal of robotics knowledge. The aim was to build and program a robot able to execute a precise action following a predefined process.

We then imagined a dog-like system capable of detecting an object, picking it up and bringing it back to us. As usual, we had a lot of ideas that we had to put into practice. Step by step. Over the 5 sessions planned for the robot's development, we organized ourselves as follows :

Over the 5 sessions planned for the robot's development, we organized ourselves as follows :
  • Initialisation : 1 session - Understanding, interpreting and taking ownership of the subject. Setting up our action plan and final objective (partly through a state graph);
  • Design - Build - Test & Verify : 3 sessions - in the form of cycles, we set up all the robot's components: movement speed, robot orientation, camera, grippers.
  • Improvements and adjustments : 1 session - Fitting the robot's various functions into a single block. We also looked for ways to optimize it, notably by improving trajectories.

If I could remember just one thing :

Adaptation : working in cycles, Jules and I were able to redefine the way the robot worked. For example, the camera we had at our disposition couldn't distinguish shapes, but it could easily detect objects based on their colors. We then trained the robot to catch a small yellow cylinder (a distinguishable color and graspable shape) instead of a real ball, as we would have liked a dog to do!
