Project overview and background

An ITEEM engineer must be able to combine his technical skills with his commercial knowledge. So, for the second year in a row, our fluid mechanics lecturer and our marketing consultant have chosen to offer second-year students a project combining their disciplines.
The aim was as follows: in a given context, develop a technical innovation for a water sprayer and come up with a concrete marketing strategy. Over a three-month period, each team of 8 students had to adapt and design a water misting/spraying system for a specific use case: food truck, market, supermarket stalls...

Our team has conceptualized a system for cooling fruit and vegetables at markets, using a battery-powered sprayer that is both ecological and economical.

The three main axes of our project were :

3D representation of the cooling system applied to a market stall
Sealing system for pipe assembly
Support system
  • Product design : To create a system that is ergonomic, economical and aesthetically pleasing. We wanted it to be demountable and adaptable to different sizes of stalls, to make it easier to set up the sprayer and also to optimize the dimensions of the product once stored. The system also had to be stable on the stall. Finally, visibility had to be guaranteed on both sides of the stall, so that the farmer could always interact with his customers.
  • Thermodynamic calculations : At regular frequencies, the cooling system diffuses a mist to keep the product fresh. We therefore had to calculate the spray frequency according to the outside temperature and the ideal temperature for preserving fruit and vegetables. We also had to calculate the water pressure in the pipes to ensure that the mist wasn't too powerful and didn't damage either the system or the produce.
  • Market study : We drew up a global marketing plan including a market study, the choice of our customer target, our strategic positioning, the communication plan, system packaging, sales targets based on production and storage costs, the geographical location of our brand and product distribution.

If I could remember just one thing :

Being able to call on the right people. We didn't have all the knowledge we needed to define the components of a cooling system like the one we had imagined. We approached our fluid mechanics and applied solid mechanics teachers to ensure that our proposed solution was validated by experts.
